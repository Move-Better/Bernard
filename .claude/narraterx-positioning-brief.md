---

## The one big idea

> **Bet against slop.**
> AI-generated voice-mimicry is flooding the internet and becoming worthless. Bernard goes the other way: it only ever uses **words you actually said**, and serves the right one to the right person at the right moment. In a feed drowning in synthetic sameness, *verifiably real + relevant* is the scarce premium.

The whole site should make a reader feel: *finally, content that's actually me — and that actually does something.*

---

## Positioning statement

For **hands-on and integrative clinicians** who want to grow a practice with content but refuse to sound like everyone else, **Bernard** turns **your own real words** into the right message for the right patient at the right time. Unlike generic AI content tools and editors (Descript, Canva, Opus, Submagic, Slate Teams), Bernard **never fabricates your voice** — it's a *librarian of what you actually said* — and it's tuned for the only thing that matters: **patients and community**, not vanity metrics.

---

## The enemy: slop

The market frame the site should set up (so Bernard is the obvious answer):

- Everyone now has "AI that makes content." It's commoditizing to zero. The result is a tidal wave of generic, soulless, interchangeable posts — **slop**.
- Clinicians feel it: they don't post because the AI version doesn't sound like them, and the generic version embarrasses them in front of their patients.
- **Bernard is the anti-slop position.** Not "better AI content" — *real* content, delivered with intelligence.

## What Bernard IS / IS NOT

| IS | IS NOT |
|---|---|
| A **librarian of your real voice** that learns your audience | A ghostwriter that imitates you |
| The **funnel** that turns real clinical voice into patients + community | A video editor / timeline tool |
| **Voice-faithful** — only ever your actual words | A brand-compliance platform for enterprise social teams (that's Slate Teams) |
| Built to **save you time** (one interview → weeks of content) | A generic AI content spinner you have to babysit |
| **Outcome-bound** — oriented toward growth, honestly measured | A vanity-metrics / engagement-bait machine |

## Who it's for

- **Primary:** hands-on & integrative providers — chiropractic, PT, OT, naturopathic, massage, acupuncture. Solo or small practice.
- **Founder-market fit:** built by a practicing chiropractor (Michael "Q" Quasney). This is a credibility weapon — lead with it.
- **Explicitly NOT for:** hospital-employed clinicians, surgeons, or enterprise social-media teams. Going narrow is the strategy, not a limitation.

## Why not just use [competitor]? (the differentiation)

The site needs a "why not X" answer because prospects will have tried these:

| They give you… | Bernard gives you… |
|---|---|
| **Descript / Opus / CapCut / Submagic** — AI that *makes* content | content that is *verifiably yours* and aimed at *your* patients |
| **Canva / Adobe Express / Slate Teams** — brand-consistent templates at scale | not brand compliance — *voice fidelity*: the words are real, the aim is smart |
| Generic "sounds like you" mimicry | a compounding library of what you genuinely said, that only gets sharper with use |

**The moat, in one line for the site:** *The longer you use Bernard, the more it sounds like you and knows your practice — because it's built from your real voice and your real results. No competitor can copy that.*

## Voice & tone for the SITE ITSELF (eat the dog food)

The marketing site must *sound like the promise*: real, not sloppy.
- Direct, peer-to-peer, opinionated. Plain language. **Anti-jargon, anti-hype.**
- No "revolutionary AI-powered synergy." If a sentence could be on any SaaS site, cut it.
- Talk like a clinician talks to another clinician. Specific > sweeping.

---

## What NOT to say (anti-patterns — these undercut the whole position)

- ❌ "AI writes your content for you." (That's the slop framing we're *against*. Say: *it surfaces your real words*.)
- ❌ "It sounds just like you!" as imitation. (Say: *it only uses words you actually said* — realness, not mimicry.)
- ❌ Leading with the editor / features. (Lead with the **outcome** and the **realness**.)
- ❌ "Guaranteed more patients." (Honest claim: *oriented toward and measured on practice growth* — we can't own the algorithm.)
- ❌ Anything that makes a clinician picture more screen time. (Always: *less work, not more*.)

## Suggested site structure (informs the build task)

1. **Hero** — the one big idea + a real-voice example side-by-side with generic slop.
2. **The slop problem** — name the enemy; make them nod.
3. **How it works** — instruction-first: *you have one real conversation → it learns your voice → it serves your actual words as the right post for the right patient.* (Not "look at our editor.")
4. **Why it compounds** — the moat, in plain language.
5. **Who it's for** — the vertical, the founder-market-fit credibility.
6. **Proof** — Book screenshot, voice before/after, case study.
7. **Pricing** — ($149 / $299 / $499 per revenue roadmap).
8. **CTA** — book a demo (Calendly) / start trial.
