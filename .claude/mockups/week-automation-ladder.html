Bernard · /week · P1-2 from the 2026-07-22 UX pain check

The automation ladder looks like a toggle. It isn't one.

PostHog logged 4 dead clicks on “Auto-approve routine” and “Run by goals” — staff trying to change their automation mode by clicking the stage they want. They can't: the mode is earned, not chosen. Nothing in the app sets it, by design (“Bernard asks; the user grants”). The shape is teaching the wrong affordance.

Proposed

Stays read-only · reads as progress

Three ways to say “this is a rung you've reached,” not “this is a control you operate.” All keep it display-only. Pick one — or mix (e.g. A's pills with C's “Stage 1 of 3”).

What changed in all three
  • The bordered track around the stages is gone — that container is the segmented-control signature.
  • No stage reuses the View toggle's solid-primary pill as its resting active state (A keeps it, but only inside a numbered, connected stepper that nothing clickable in Bernard looks like).
  • Stages ahead read as unearned (dim / dashed / absent) rather than as unselected options.
  • Markup moves from role="group" to <nav aria-label="Automation progress"> with aria-current="step" — screen readers stop hearing a control group too.
  • Still zero interactivity, and no tooltip band-aid. Same data, honest shape.

Confirmed against the code before mocking: trust_stage is written once at onboarding (approve_all) and has no setter anywhere in the app — the spec's graduation model is “signal = agreement, not time … Bernard asks; the user grants.” So read-only is correct; only the affordance is wrong. Wiring it into a real picker would contradict the design.
