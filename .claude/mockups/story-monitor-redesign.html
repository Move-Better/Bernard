One of the major steps people miss in evaluating knee pain with running is they don't go watch the thing that's creating the pain.

That's the clinical principle that shapes everything we do at Move Better. If knee pain happens when you run, we're going to go run. Whatever movement is creating the pain, we'll watch you do it as close to real life as possible.

From there, our evaluation is specific. We watch cadence, head motion, foot strike — and then we watch them skip. We need people using their hips to create hip flexion to run. But sometimes there's a lot of drive coming from the calf and the quad — which is where a fair amount of this knee pain comes from.

The calf needs to be the suspension. The hips need to be the motor. That's the shift — and most of the time, nobody watched them run long enough to find it.
