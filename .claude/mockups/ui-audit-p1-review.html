Why this direction

The severity-ramp principle you set is that amber = “an action only you can take is waiting.” “In review” is exactly that — approve the words. “Needs draft” isn't: Bernard (or one click) produces the draft, so it's a backlog/to-do state, not an alert. Painting it amber spends the board's loudest signal on the one state that doesn't need you yet.

Making it a quiet dashed/neutral chip keeps it visible as “not started” while letting the eye land straight on the real amber. It's a cardState() change plus one pill/rail style — no new tokens, no layout shift.

Why this direction

Keep pinned only what you operate constantly: search and the quick-filter tabs. Fold the sort + advanced-filters triggers into compact icon buttons on the search row, and let the active-filter chips flow inline with content — they only exist when a filter is on, and when one is on you're scrolled to the results anyway. Net: ~70px back, and two full story rows visible on first paint instead of half of one.

The desktop header is unaffected — it has the room. This is a mobile-breakpoint change to the sticky stack only.

Option A · Trim the UI
Staff + Topic + Start stay; voice, recipe, and levers collapse under “More options.” Matches “two things” literally. Cost: overrides the deliberate always-visible-voice decision — worth a gut-check that voice is genuinely fine one tap away.

Why I lean B

The contradiction is real, but the cheapest honest fix is usually to make the words match the screen rather than hide a field someone deliberately surfaced. Voice is set once and shapes everything — that's a defensible thing to keep in front of the user. If you'd rather the screen feel like two decisions, Option A is right and I'll wire the collapse — just confirming you're okay demoting voice before I do.
