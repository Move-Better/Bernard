One pathway per media type: videos open the video editor — inside the publish shell

Photos already open SlideEditor (editing canvas wrapped in the publish shell). Video pieces are the exception — they land on a caption‑only screen with no editing. This change makes a Reel open the real video editor with the same Approve / Schedule chrome around it.

Philip, on the Reel publish screen: “No control over video editing, subtitling, or design of reels within this screen.”

Why this shape: it's the exact mirror of the photo pathway. A photo piece already opens its editor inside the publish shell; this gives video pieces the same deal. No new second screen, no jumping between an editor and a publisher — one surface, chosen by the media on the piece.
One caveat to confirm: when a Reel's video is a raw uploaded clip (no transcript, like Philip's here), the subtitle is baked into the file's pixels. The editor can lay new Bernard captions over it, but can't erase the original burned‑in text. Clips Bernard generated from an interview have full word‑level subtitle editing.
