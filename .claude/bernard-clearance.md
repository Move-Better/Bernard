# "BERNARD" Brand Clearance Research

**Date:** 2026-06-07
**Subject:** Viability of renaming Bernard → "Bernard" (displayed brand), likely domain `withbernard.ai`
**Product:** Captures an expert's authentic voice via voice/video interview → brand-aligned marketing content (social, blog, email, video clips). Beachhead = hands-on/integrative healthcare providers (chiro, PT, OT, massage, acupuncture, naturopaths); long-term horizontal across expert-led service businesses.

**Method:** WebSearch + WebFetch. USPTO TESS / official trademark APIs were unreachable from this environment (404 on the API path, 403 on Justia search, and the TESS UI is JS/CAPTCHA-walled), so trademark findings below are **inferred from secondary sources** (Justia/Trademarkia surfaced via search), not confirmed registry reads. Treat the trademark section as a signal, not a legal clearance.

- **Risk read:** low-to-unknown. Whatever bernard.com is, it is not visibly competing in our class. The apex `.com` being taken just means we can't use it — not that it's a collision.

## 3. Bernard Health (the dangerous adjacency)

**Confirmed, real, and squarely in healthcare-adjacent territory.**
- **Bernard Health** — Nashville, TN benefits brokerage + HR-software company. Parent of **BerniePortal** (all-in-one HRIS + payroll for SMBs) and **Alpine** (COBRA/HSA/FSA admin). Also BernieForms (health questionnaires).
- Scale: estimates vary by source — ~$7.1M revenue (2025), ~28–70 employees, ~$2–3M raised. Mid-tier, not a giant, but **established, multi-product, and well-indexed for "Bernard" + health/benefits searches.** Note Bernard Benefits / Bernard Healthcare Financial Planning were acquired by Arthur J. Gallagher (2023), further entrenching the "Bernard" + healthcare association.

**Confusion assessment for a healthcare-marketing "Bernard":**
- Bernard Health's domain is `bernardhealth.com`; products brand as **BerniePortal / Alpine**, not "Bernard" standalone. So *visual* brand collision on the exact string is partial.
- **BUT** the adjacency is real and uncomfortable: our beachhead is **healthcare providers**, and Bernard Health sells **into employers/HR in healthcare-benefits**. A chiropractor or clinic owner Googling "Bernard healthcare" today lands on Bernard Health. Our buyers and theirs are different (clinic marketing vs. employer benefits/HR), so it's **adjacent, not overlapping** — but it muddies SEO, paid-search, and word-of-mouth precisely in the vertical we're entering.
- Trademark-wise, classes differ (theirs = insurance/HR-software services; ours = marketing-content SaaS), which reduces formal-conflict risk — but examiners and the market both weight "same word + same broad industry (healthcare)" heavily.

**Risk read (axis b):** **MEDIUM-to-HIGH.** Not a blocker, but this is the single biggest concern. "Bernard" + healthcare already has an incumbent meaning. Launching a healthcare-vertical product called "Bernard" walks into an occupied semantic space. The horizontal long-term vision (expert-led service businesses generally) *dilutes* this risk over time, but the **beachhead is exactly where the collision is worst.**

## 4. Trademark signal (best-effort, NOT a legal opinion)

**Caveat:** USPTO TESS API returned 404, Justia search returned 403, and no official registry read succeeded. Below is inferred from search-surfaced records only.

- **No confirmed live "BERNARD" word mark in software/SaaS/marketing classes (9 / 35 / 42)** surfaced in searches. That's an *absence of evidence*, not evidence of absence — the registry wasn't directly searchable.
- Records that did surface are unrelated: "SAINT BERNARD'S" (beverages/cosmetics), a "Bernard B. Kohan" filing for an online buyers-guide service, assorted personal-name filings. None are a clean "BERNARD" mark in our lanes.
- **Bernard Health** very likely holds marks around "BERNARD HEALTH" / "BERNIEPORTAL" in insurance/HR classes — not directly our classes, but the healthcare overlap (see §3) is the part a trademark attorney would scrutinize hardest.
- "Bernard" is a **common given name/surname**, which generally makes it harder to claim broad exclusive rights and easier for multiple registrants to coexist across classes — a mild point in favor. But common-name marks also get less protection *for us*, meaning weaker defensibility.

**Risk read (axis c):** **MEDIUM (and under-measured).** No obvious blocking registration found in our classes, but (1) the search was not a real registry clearance, and (2) the healthcare adjacency to Bernard Health is the live question. **A paid USPTO/full-knockout search by counsel is required before committing** — do not treat this section as clearance.

## 5. Domain options

- **`withbernard.ai`** — the working plan. Pattern is clean and increasingly common for AI products ("with___" connotes a companion/assistant, which fits a voice-interview concierge). Confirmed `bernard.ai` and `bernard.com` are unavailable (parked-for-sale / taken), so `with-` prefix is the reasonable route. *Could not confirm withbernard.ai is itself unregistered* — direct fetch refused connection, which is ambiguous (could be unregistered, could be parked/blocking bots). **Verify availability via a registrar WHOIS before assuming it's free.**
- **`bernardhq.com` — TAKEN** (active "Bernard, AI OS for the trades"). Rules out the `___hq` pattern and is itself a same-trope adjacency worth noting.
- **`bernard-ai.org` — TAKEN** (open-source frontend).
- **`hellobernard.ai` / `mybernard.ai` — TAKEN.** So `hello-` and `my-` prefixes are gone.
- Other patterns to check (likely many taken given the trope): `trybernard.com/.ai`, `getbernard.com/.ai`, `bernard.app`, `bernard.io`, `heybernard.com/.ai`, `usebernard.com`. Given how crowded "Bernard AI" is, expect most short prefixes to be claimed — `withbernard.ai` may genuinely be one of the last clean ones, which is itself a signal the namespace is congested.

## VERDICT

**"Bernard" is VIABLE but RISKY — not blocked, but not a clean runway either. Proceed only after a real trademark knockout search, and go in eyes-open about a congested namespace.**

| Axis | Risk | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **(a) Existing AI/SaaS "Bernard" products** | **MEDIUM-LOW** | No funded competitor in voice→marketing-content. But "AI assistant named Bernard" is a crowded trope (hellobernard.ai, bernardhq.com, a Genius ChatLLM app, etc.) → noisy brand search, weak SEO ownership, harder differentiation by name alone. |
| **(b) Bernard Health / healthcare adjacency** | **MEDIUM-HIGH** | Real, established healthcare/benefits company already owns "Bernard + healthcare" mindshare. Different buyer (HR/benefits vs. clinic marketing) and different products (BerniePortal/Alpine, not "Bernard" standalone) keep it adjacent-not-overlapping — but our **beachhead is exactly the vertical where the collision is worst.** This is the top concern. |
| **(c) Trademark** | **MEDIUM (under-measured)** | No blocking mark found in classes 9/35/42, but the registry was not directly searchable and the Bernard Health healthcare overlap is the live question. Requires counsel. |

### Recommendation
1. **Don't kill it, but treat the healthcare adjacency as the deciding factor.** If the beachhead were any other vertical, "Bernard" would be a comfortable MEDIUM-LOW. The healthcare beachhead is what pushes overall risk up. Weigh how long you'll stay healthcare-only before going horizontal — the longer in healthcare, the longer you live next to Bernard Health.
2. **Before committing any spend:** (a) run a real USPTO full-knockout / counsel clearance in classes 9/35/42 *and* healthcare classes (44/insurance), specifically vetting against Bernard Health's marks; (b) WHOIS-verify `withbernard.ai` is actually registrable; (c) test brand-search reality — Google "Bernard AI" and "Bernard healthcare" and see how buried our product would be.
3. **If you proceed:** lean hard on the `withbernard.ai` lockup as the canonical brand (the bare word "Bernard" is too contested to own), and consider a disambiguating descriptor in the wordmark for the healthcare phase to separate from Bernard Health.
4. **Lower-risk alternative worth a parallel look:** because the "Bernard AI" namespace is congested *and* the healthcare incumbent exists, a more distinctive coined name would clear faster and own SEO outright. If naming is still open, that tradeoff (warmth/memorability of "Bernard" vs. clean ownership of a coined mark) is the real decision.
