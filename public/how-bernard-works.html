How it works · the mechanisms

How Bernard actually does what it promises

Talk for a few minutes, get a week of on-brand content — it sounds too good. This page explains, in plain terms, the real machinery behind each promise: how Bernard learns your voice, scores it, finds clips in long video, writes captions, designs graphics, plans the week, and publishes — plus how to use each part, and the guardrails that keep it trustworthy.

1 The core idea

One principle runs through everything Bernard does.

Bernard never invents your expertise. Every post starts from something a clinician really said — captured in an interview or a video. Bernard's job is to produce from that raw material: it learns the clinician's voice, grounds every draft in the real transcript, scores the result for faithfulness, and waits for a human "yes" before anything publishes. That's why the output sounds genuine instead of like generic AI.

What's under the hood. Bernard stands on best-in-class AI — Anthropic's Claude does the writing and editorial judgment, OpenAI's Whisper turns speech into text, and ElevenLabs powers the optional spoken-voice clone. Bernard's own work is the orchestration around them: learning your voice, grounding drafts in real transcripts, scoring fidelity, metering the output, and keeping each clinic's data separate.

2 Your voice — the heart of Bernard

How content comes out sounding like the clinician, and how that's verified.

How Bernard learns your voice

Promise: the more you use it, the more it sounds like you.

How it works

Bernard builds a voice profile for each clinician from three signals that accumulate over time:

  • Your real phrases. Whenever you approve a piece, Bernard lifts the genuine sentences out of it and banks them as "voice phrases" for that clinician. Phrases you approve again rise in weight, so your most characteristic phrasing floats to the top. It keeps your literal words — not an AI paraphrase — so they can be traced in future drafts.
  • How you edit. When you rewrite an AI draft, Bernard compares the before and after and summarizes the patterns ("cuts hedging, prefers short sentences") into voice notes that steer the next draft.
  • Your thinking. After each interview, Bernard distills your philosophy and the way you frame things into a searchable "practice memory," so later pieces can echo how you actually explain ideas.
Try it

Just use Bernard — the profile builds passively as you do interviews and approve/edit pieces. On a clinician's profile you can hit Refresh voice notes to re-learn from recent edits.

How a caption or post is written

Promise: a finished, on-voice caption from raw material.

How it works

When you draft a piece, Bernard hands the writer model a brief that separates what from how:

  • What to say comes from the actual transcript of the interview or clip — the model paraphrases what was really said and is told not to invent claims.
  • How to say it comes from your voice profile: your phrases (rhythm and word choice), your editing patterns, and the relevant bits of your practice memory.
  • Context comes from any active campaign and the channel itself — an Instagram caption, a LinkedIn post, and a Google Business post each get tailored guidance and angle.

Claude writes the draft; carousels also get a slide-by-slide plan.

Try it

In Your Week, open a planned item that "needs draft" and click Draft. The caption is written on the spot — drafts are generated only when you ask for them, never in bulk.

How your voice gets a score

Promise: a number that tells you how true-to-voice a piece is.

How it works

After a piece is generated, a separate AI "judge" reads it and rates it on four things:

  • Did it stick to what was said? — checked against the clip's transcript. Inventing or distorting a claim tanks this score.
  • Does it sound like you? — checked against your voice phrases, in whatever register you used. It does not reward jargon — a warm, plain caption scores just as well as a clinical one.
  • Is it natural? — does it read like a person, not a content mill.
  • Is it tight? — no padding; the headline and caption each earn their place.

The four are averaged into one fidelity score shown on the piece. Behind the scenes, Bernard also runs a standing quality gate: captions are scored regularly against real transcripts and the average must stay above a bar before any change to how Bernard writes can ship.

Why you can trust it

The score is graded against the clinician's real words, not a vibe — and the scoring itself is validated so it measures faithfulness, not jargon-density. It exists to catch drift before a piece ever reaches you.

Your spoken voice (optional)

Promise: Bernard can speak in the clinician's own voice.

How it works

On the Voice Training page a clinician reads a short (~3–4 minute) script. Bernard creates a private voice clone (via ElevenLabs) tied to that person, and can then read text aloud in their voice — most concretely, to generate a short, personalized pre-visit voice message.

Honest scope: the spoken clone powers read-aloud and pre-visit messages today. It is not used to narrate published reels — those carry on-screen captions, not synthetic narration. And it's opt-in: nothing is cloned unless a clinician records the sample, and it can be revoked anytime.

Try it

Go to Voice Training and record the script. Revoke or re-clone whenever you like.

3 Moment Miner — finding clips in a long video

Drop in an hour of footage; get the few moments worth posting.

How it works

The key insight: Bernard reads the video, it doesn't just chop it up.

  1. It pulls the audio and transcribes it (Whisper), keeping timestamps.
  2. An editorial AI reads the full timestamped transcript and selects the strongest standalone moments — a vivid teaching beat, a quotable line, a counterintuitive insight — skipping filler and repetition. It aligns each clip to whole sentences (roughly 8–60 seconds) so nothing starts mid-thought. The hard part is selection, not cutting.
  3. A second pass rates each moment 0–100 for "quotability" (how well it would stand alone as a Reel) and tags it by type — coaching cue, patient breakthrough, hook, credibility, insight, technique, or story.
  4. Your feed shows the moments ranked best-first, each with its quote, score, and tag.

You can kick this off on a video yourself, or Bernard quietly scans new uploads in the background.

Why you can trust it

This is detection only — Bernard finds and ranks moments, but a human decides what's kept and what ships. Every clip is the clinician's real words on camera; Bernard never generates or fakes footage. The principle: automate the labor of finding moments, never the judgment of what represents the clinic.

Try it
  1. Open Moment Miner → the Uncut footage tab and click Find moments on a video.
  2. Detection runs for a few minutes (you'll see it working).
  3. Switch to Ready to review, skim the ranked feed, filter by type or staff member.
  4. Save the keepers, or Review & trim one to fine-tune it in the editor.

4 The Reel editor — clip to finished reel

Turn a raw clip into a captioned, on-brand vertical video.

How it works

The editor is a live preview with simple controls. When you export, a server renders the final video (with ffmpeg):

  • Format — Reel (9:16), Square (1:1), or Portrait (4:5) from the same clip.
  • Trim & reframe — set the in/out point, zoom and pan, with optional Ken Burns motion.
  • Captions — generated from the transcript and burned into the video, with word-by-word "karaoke" highlighting in your brand colour; choose Pop or Fade. You can click any word to fix it.
  • Brand grade ("vibe") — colour presets or fine sliders; one tap applies your saved brand look.
  • Overlays — add titles, lower-thirds, or callouts, positioned by drag.

The finished video is saved to your Library and can be scheduled as a post, kept as b-roll, or exported in ad sizes. Your edits autosave, so you can resume on any device.

Try it

From a moment, click Review & trim (or open a clip from the Library). Set the trim, pick a format, toggle captions, choose a vibe, then Export → Render & send.

5 Photos & carousels

A plain photo becomes an on-brand graphic with a headline.

How it works

You compose live in the browser; the published image is baked on the server so it carries your brand exactly. Bernard derives your palette automatically — it reads your Brand Kit colours and picks the darkest as the "ink" and the lightest as the "paper" for backgrounds and text, so templates look on-brand without you choosing hex codes.

In the editor you can: add or swap a photo (AI "picks," a describe-the-shot search, your Library, or an upload); place and style headline text (brand fonts, brand-swatch colours, drag to position); apply a "colorist" grade (presets, or describe a look and an AI turns it into settings); control fit, zoom and blur; and build multi-slide carousels. Generate from brand can even produce template looks for you.

Why you can trust it

What you approve is re-rendered on the server into the actual published image (the original photo is preserved underneath), so the live output matches your design — it's a real render, not a screenshot of the preview.

Try it

Open a piece that has a photo → the editor. Pick a template or Generate from brand, set your headline, tune the look, add slides for a carousel, and save.

6 The guided interview & transcription

Where it all starts: a natural conversation that captures expertise.

The guided interview

How it works

It's a streaming AI conversation. Each question adapts to your last answer — it re-probes shallow answers, follows interesting threads, and wraps up when it has enough. You can speak (your browser transcribes as you talk) or type. The running Q&A is saved continuously, with a local backup, and becomes the transcript that feeds everything downstream. The raw audio is also kept — purely as future voice-training material, not to make the transcript.

Try it

Create → Interview, pick a topic, and talk. Don't over-prepare — the interviewer drives. Finish when it wraps up, and Bernard takes it from there.

Transcription

How it works

Uploaded and recorded media — clips, voice memos, even hour-plus seminars — are transcribed by OpenAI Whisper. Long talks are split, transcribed in parallel, and stitched back together; timestamps are saved so clip renders never have to re-transcribe. (Live interview answers use your browser's own speech-to-text for instant feedback.)

7 Your Week — planning & cadence

A balanced week of content at sensible times — without micromanaging.

How it works
  • Each interview seeds a plan. Bernard breaks it into planned "pieces" — one per channel and angle (an Instagram hook, a LinkedIn insight, a Google Business post…). A planner AI chooses the strongest pieces per channel and writes a one-line brief for each, validated so it only ever references a real interview.
  • Cadence is computed, not hand-set. Bernard looks at the channels you've enabled and gives each a posts-per-week target from a sensible default (which is editable). Enable a channel and it automatically gets a cadence — or switch to Manual and set your own.
  • It's a drip, not a dump. One big interview doesn't flood your feeds. Bernard releases pieces up to each channel's weekly target and banks the rest as backlog, draining it over the following weeks. Posts are spread across non-quiet weekdays at good hours, in your timezone.
  • A piece appears on Your Week once it's been slotted for the current week. Captions are written only when you open a piece to draft it.
Try it
  1. Open Your Week and Draft the pieces you want.
  2. Read each one and click "Sounds like me" to approve it.
  3. Click "Schedule approved" to slot the approved set.
  4. To change pace: enable/disable channels in Settings (Auto mode), or set targets yourself (Manual). Quiet days and timezone live there too.

8 Publishing

One idea, posted everywhere — automatically, but never without permission.

How it works

Bernard connects to your own accounts through a posting service (the default plus a newer option), using credentials you provide that are stored encrypted. When you approve and schedule a piece, Bernard hands each channel's version to the service to post at its planned time. The same weekly plan fans out across channels, and Google Business posts can fan out to each clinic location. For video and Google-Business items there's also an automatic publisher that posts approved content on schedule.

Why you can trust it

The hard rule: nothing publishes without your yes. Bernard drafts and schedules; you approve. Your connected-account secrets are encrypted at rest, and each clinic only ever posts to its own connected accounts.

Try it

Connect your accounts once in Settings → Integrations. After that, approving and scheduling in Your Week is all it takes.

9 Seeing what works

Bernard learns from results, not guesses.

How it works

Bernard connects, read-only, to Google Search Console, Google Analytics, and Google Business Profile to pull real search and traffic performance (per-location for Google Business). That data informs topic suggestions and where to aim — so the plan reflects what's actually landing, not a hunch.

10 Trust & safety — the guardrails

The part clinics ask about most, in one place.

Nothing is invented

Every post traces to something a clinician really said; the writer is grounded in real transcripts and told not to fabricate claims.

No AI-generated video, ever

Bernard never creates synthetic footage or fake talking heads. Clips are your real clinician on camera. Finding moments is automated; deciding what represents the clinic is not.

No patient stories

Content stays at the level of education and quick wins — never individual patient outcomes or identifiable details.

Voice is checked

Every piece is scored for fidelity to the clinician's real words and voice, to catch drift before you see it.

A human always approves

Drafting and scheduling are automated; publishing requires your explicit "yes."

Your data is walled off

Each clinic is its own workspace. Every request is authenticated and scoped, so one clinic can't see another's content.

Credentials are encrypted

Connected-account secrets are stored with strong (AES-256) encryption — never as plain settings.

Backed up

The database is snapshotted automatically every day, with independent manual backups as well.
